# Experimental Plan: Verifying the Loss-Landscape Theory of Mode Collapse

## Purpose

This document outlines an experimental program designed to verify the central claims of the loss-landscape theory for category (a) SOC samplers developed in `soc_loss_foundations.md`. Unlike prior experimental efforts which cataloged mode collapse phenomena broadly, this program is organized around specific theoretical predictions, with each experimental goal designed to test (or falsify) a concrete claim.

The experimental program is organized into five sequential goals, each building on the previous.

-----

## Theoretical Predictions Under Test

1. **Prediction P1 (Stability).** For any proper subset $S \subsetneq {1, \ldots, K}$ with $\mathcal{L}_S^* < \tau^*$, the $S$-collapsed controller $\theta_S^*$ is a local minimum of the training loss. Gradient descent from initializations near $\theta_S^*$ remains near $\theta_S^*$.
1. **Prediction P2 (Predictivity ranking).** Among all possible collapsed subsets, those with lower $\mathcal{L}_S^*$ are more likely to be stable attractors reached by training from random initialization.
1. **Prediction P3 (Hessian positivity at stable collapsed states).** At a stable collapsed controller, $\nabla^2 \mathcal{L}(\theta_S^*)$ is positive semi-definite, with strict positivity on the revival subspace $\Theta_{S^c}$.
1. **Prediction P4 (Hessian indefiniteness at unstable collapsed states).** At a collapsed controller with $\mathcal{L}_S^* > \tau^*$ for which the theorem gives no stability guarantee, the Hessian may exhibit negative eigenvalues, particularly in revival directions.
1. **Prediction P5 (Assumption L5 consequence).** At any collapsed controller, controller variations along revival directions satisfy $P_1 \geq c_u |\delta\alpha|^2$ where $P_1 = \mathbb{E}[\int|\delta u|^2 dt]$. Equivalently, the slope of $P_1$ vs $|\delta\alpha|^2$ is bounded below.
1. **Prediction P6 (Step-size escape).** Gradient descent with step size larger than $2/\lambda_{\max}(\nabla^2 \mathcal{L}(\theta_S^*))$ may escape even theoretically-stable collapsed states, as a consequence of discrete-dynamics instability rather than loss-landscape instability.

-----

## Shared Infrastructure and Constants

All five goals share the following setup:

**Diffusion schedule:** VE-SDE with $g(t) = \sigma_{\min}(\sigma_{\max}/\sigma_{\min})^t$, $\sigma_{\min} = 0.01$, $\sigma_{\max} = 3.0$, giving $\sigma(1) = \sqrt{\int_0^1 g^2,dt} \approx 2.0$ and $g_{\max} = \sigma_{\max} = 3.0$.

**Sampler implementations.** All experiments are run on category (a) SOC sampler implementations. Specific architectures, loss formulations, and training schedules are documented separately in `category_a_samplers.md`. The plan below is written in terms of “the sampler” abstractly; any category (a) sampler satisfying the theoretical assumptions L1-L5 is a valid test-bed.

We recommend running all experiments on **multiple independent category (a) samplers** (at least two — e.g., Adjoint Sampling and Adjoint Schrödinger Bridge Sampler). Cross-sampler consistency is itself an additional robustness check: the theory is architecture-agnostic, so results should be qualitatively consistent across samplers.

Hyperparameters (network depth, width, learning rate, batch size, optimizer) are taken from each sampler’s reference configuration and not fixed globally. This plan specifies only *training-length budgets in epochs* and *evaluation-sample sizes*, which are the quantities relevant to the theoretical predictions.

**Sampling for evaluation.** $N_{\mathrm{eval}} = 10{,}000$ trajectories for mode-weight estimation unless otherwise specified.

**Training budgets (in epochs).** Since 2D benchmarks are light, modest training is sufficient:

- Pre-training on restricted targets: **500 epochs** per subset.
- Stability run on full target: **200 epochs** from pre-trained init.
- Step-size continuation (Goal 5): **300 epochs**.

**What an epoch means.** One epoch = one batch of freshly sampled trajectories + forward-simulated terminal costs + loss computation + gradient step. There is no finite training dataset — trajectories are generated by the current policy at the start of each epoch. The specific batch size per epoch is sampler-dependent and taken from each sampler’s reference configuration.

**No inner-loop evaluation during training.** Training proceeds without mid-run evaluation passes; this keeps epochs cheap. Evaluation happens separately after training (for final-state measurements in all goals) or at coarse intervals only when stability classification requires mid-training signal (Goals 1 and 5 — see per-goal specifications).

### Benchmark Suite

Three 2D benchmarks with $K = 5$ modes each, designed to isolate one hardness axis per benchmark.

#### Benchmark W5: Weight Heterogeneity

**Target.** Five isotropic Gaussians at vertices of a regular pentagon (radius $R = 8.0$), covariance $\sigma_{\mathrm{mode}}^2 I$ with $\sigma_{\mathrm{mode}} = 0.8$, unequal weights with geometric decay:
$$w_k = \frac{r^{k-1}}{\sum_{j=1}^5 r^{j-1}}, \quad k = 1, \ldots, 5$$
with $r = 0.5$, yielding $(w_1, w_2, w_3, w_4, w_5) \approx (0.52, 0.26, 0.13, 0.06, 0.03)$. Mode positions: $\mu_k = R(\cos(2\pi k/5), \sin(2\pi k/5))$. Adjacent-mode distance $\approx 9.4$, giving $\rho_{\mathrm{sep}} \approx 4.7$.

**Hardness axis.** Weight imbalance. Modes 4 and 5 are expensive to include.

**Predictions.** Subsets containing only high-weight modes (${1}$, ${1,2}$, ${1,2,3}$) have low $\mathcal{L}_S^*$; subsets containing low-weight modes (${4,5}$, ${3,4,5}$) have higher $\mathcal{L}_S^*$.

#### Benchmark C5: Covariance Heterogeneity

**Target.** Five Gaussians at pentagon vertices (same positions as W5), equal weights $w_k = 1/5$, heterogeneous covariances:

- Modes 1, 2, 3: wide isotropic, $\sigma_{\mathrm{wide}} = 0.8$.
- Mode 4: tight isotropic, $\sigma_{\mathrm{tight}} = 0.15$.
- Mode 5: anisotropic, $\mathrm{diag}(\sigma_{\mathrm{wide}}^2, \sigma_{\mathrm{tight}}^2)$.

**Hardness axis.** Covariance heterogeneity. Modes 4 and 5 require sharp concentration or anisotropy.

**Predictions.** Subsets of only wide isotropic modes have low $\mathcal{L}_S^*$; subsets containing mode 4 or 5 have higher $\mathcal{L}_S^*$.

#### Benchmark B5: Barrier Heterogeneity

**Target.** Five isotropic Gaussians ($\sigma_{\mathrm{mode}} = 0.8$, equal weights $w_k = 1/5$) in graph-structured configuration:

- Cluster modes 1, 2, 3: positions $(-3, 0)$, $(0, 0)$, $(3, 0)$. Pairwise separation $3.0$, giving $\rho_{\mathrm{sep,near}} \approx 1.5$.
- Isolated modes 4, 5: positions $(0, 12)$, $(0, -12)$. Distance to cluster $\approx 12$, giving $\rho_{\mathrm{sep,far}} \approx 6.0$.

**Hardness axis.** Barrier heterogeneity. Reaching modes 4 or 5 requires long-distance drift.

**Predictions.** Cluster-internal subsets (${1,2,3}$, ${1,2}$, ${2,3}$) have low $\mathcal{L}_S^*$; subsets containing modes 4 or 5 have higher $\mathcal{L}_S^*$.

### Benchmark Summary

|Benchmark|$K$|Axis tested             |Predicted easy subsets     |Predicted hard subsets     |
|---------|---|------------------------|---------------------------|---------------------------|
|W5       |5  |Weight imbalance        |${1}$, ${1,2}$, ${1,2,3}$  |${4,5}$, ${3,4,5}$         |
|C5       |5  |Covariance heterogeneity|${1}$, ${1,2}$, ${1,2,3}$  |Any subset with mode 4 or 5|
|B5       |5  |Barrier heterogeneity   |${1,2,3}$, ${1,2}$, ${2,3}$|Any subset with mode 4 or 5|

## Goal 1: Stability Test — Do Collapsed States Remain Stable?

**Claim tested:** Prediction P1.

**Central question.** If we initialize training at a mode-collapsed state, does training stay there? And does stability depend on $\mathcal{L}_S^*$ as the theorem predicts?

### Stability Metric

**Evaluation protocol.** During stability runs, evaluate mode weights at coarse intervals: every $20$ epochs. Evaluation consists of sampling $N_{\mathrm{eval}} = 10{,}000$ trajectories from the current policy $Q_{\theta_e}$ and counting terminal-basin memberships to estimate $\alpha_k^{(\theta_e)}$. Evaluation is outside the training loop — it does not consume training gradients.

**Primary: Mode Weight Deviation (MWD).** At evaluation epoch $e$:
$$\mathrm{MWD}(e) := \max_{k \in {1,\ldots,K}} |\alpha_k^{(\theta_e)} - \alpha_k^{(S)}|.$$

**Classification thresholds:**

- **Stable:** $\mathrm{MWD}(e) < \epsilon_{\mathrm{tol}} = 0.05$ for all evaluation points $e \in [e_{\mathrm{warmup}}, e_{\mathrm{max}}]$, with $e_{\mathrm{warmup}} = 20$, $e_{\mathrm{max}} = 200$.
- **Escaped:** $\mathrm{MWD}(e) > \epsilon_{\mathrm{escape}} = 0.15$ for at least $3$ consecutive evaluation points (i.e., spanning $60$ epochs) before $e_{\mathrm{max}}$.
- **Ambiguous:** neither condition met.

**Secondary metrics:** Dead-mode revival epoch $e^*$ (first evaluation at which $\alpha_k > 0.05$ for some $k \notin S$), per-mode weight trajectories at evaluation points.

### Subset Selection

**Exhaustive coverage.** For each benchmark, test all proper non-trivial subsets:

- $|S| = 1$: all $5$ subsets.
- $|S| = 2$: all $\binom{5}{2} = 10$ subsets.
- $|S| = 3$: all $\binom{5}{3} = 10$ subsets.
- $|S| = 4$: all $\binom{5}{4} = 5$ subsets.

Total: $30$ subsets per benchmark. This exhaustive coverage subsumes what would otherwise be Goal 2’s separate subset enumeration — the same pre-trained checkpoints and stability runs serve both Goal 1’s stability test and Goal 2’s predictivity analysis.

**Expected structure.** Under the theory, easy subsets (those matching predicted-easy patterns in the Benchmark Summary table) should be stable; hard subsets should be less stable. The quantitative relationship between $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ and stability is analyzed in Goal 2.

### Protocol

**Stage 1: Pre-training ($\theta_S^*$ approximation).** For each subset $S$:

1. Construct $p_S$ by re-normalizing to exclude dead modes.
1. Pre-train on $p_S$ for $500$ epochs with no mid-training evaluation.
1. Record $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$: training loss at the final epoch (or averaged over the last few epochs if loss is noisy).
1. Save the final parameter checkpoint $\theta_S^*$.

**Stage 2: Stability run.** Initialize fresh training on full target $p$ from $\theta_S^*$. Budget: $200$ epochs. Mode weights evaluated every $20$ epochs via $N_{\mathrm{eval}} = 10{,}000$ sampled trajectories (outside the training loop). Save parameter checkpoints every $40$ epochs (reused by Goals 3-5).

**Stage 3: Seed replication.** $N_{\mathrm{seed}} = 5$ independent seeds per $(S, \text{benchmark}, \text{sampler})$ triple. Report stability as fraction of seeds stable.

**Compute per sampler:** Stage 1: $30$ subsets × $500$ epochs = $15{,}000$ epochs per benchmark, $45{,}000$ across 3 benchmarks. Stage 2: $30 \times 200 \times 5 = 30{,}000$ epochs per benchmark, $90{,}000$ across 3 benchmarks. Evaluation adds $\sim 10$ evaluation passes per stability run, each generating $10{,}000$ samples — marginal overhead.

### Logged Quantities

**During training (Stages 1 and 2):** Training loss at each epoch. No other inner-loop logging.

**At Stage 2 evaluation points (every 20 epochs):** Mode weights $\alpha_k^{(\theta_e)}$ from 10k-sample estimation; MWD derived.

**Parameter checkpoints:** Every $40$ epochs during Stage 2 (reused by Goals 3-5).

**At Stage 2 endpoints:** Terminal sample scatter plots at $e \in {0, 50, 100, 200}$.

### Evaluation and Reporting

**Table 1 (Stability Matrix):** Per $(S, \text{benchmark}, \text{sampler})$, stability proportion across 5 seeds. Each benchmark-sampler combination produces a $30$-row table.

**Figure 1 (MWD Trajectories):** One subplot per benchmark-sampler pair; all 30 subsets overlaid, colored by predicted easy/hard category.

**Figure 2 (Stability by Subset Size):** For each benchmark-sampler pair, plot stability proportion stratified by $|S|$. Confound check that “smaller = more stable” doesn’t drive results.

**Figure 3 ($\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ histogram):** Distribution of measured $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ values across all 30 subsets per benchmark-sampler. Shows the spread available for the threshold analysis in Goal 2.

### Falsification Criteria

- **F1.1 (Complete stability).** Every tested subset is stable across all seeds.
- **F1.2 (Complete instability).** No tested subsets are stable, including those predicted easy.
- **F1.3 (Inverted predictivity).** Hard subsets more stable than easy ones (to be quantified in Goal 2).
- **F1.4 (Sampler inconsistency).** Results disagree qualitatively between samplers (when running on multiple category (a) samplers).

**Success criterion:** Easy subsets stable with high probability; hard subsets systematically less stable; results qualitatively consistent across any samplers tested.

### Ablation Axes

- **1.1 Initialization noise:** $\theta_S^* + \eta,\xi$ with $\eta \in {0, 0.01, 0.05, 0.1}$. Probes basin-of-attraction size.
- **1.2 Mode separation:** overall scale giving $\rho_{\mathrm{sep}} \in {3, 4, 5, 7}$.
- **1.3 Cross-sampler consistency:** Direct comparison of results on identical subsets across different category (a) samplers.

## Goal 2: Predictivity Ranking — Does $\mathcal{L}_S^*$ Predict Which Subsets are Stable?

**Claim tested:** Prediction P2.

**Central question.** Does ranking subsets by $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ correctly predict which are stable vs unstable? Is there an observable threshold separating the two regimes?

**Relation to Goal 1.** Goal 2 is an analysis pass on the data collected in Goal 1 — no new training required. Goal 1’s exhaustive coverage of all 30 subsets per benchmark provides the inputs: for each subset, we have (a) its measured $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ from Stage 1, and (b) its stability classification from Stage 2. Goal 2 asks whether (a) predicts (b).

### Analysis

For each (benchmark, sampler) combination:

**Step 1: Ranking comparison.** Rank the 30 subsets by $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ from low to high. Compute the stability proportion $p_S^{\mathrm{stab}}$ for each subset (fraction of 5 seeds classified stable in Goal 1).

**Step 2: Threshold detection.** Walk through the $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$-sorted list. Identify the threshold $\hat{\tau}$ that best separates stable from unstable subsets, e.g., the value maximizing classification accuracy where “predicted stable” means $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^* < \hat{\tau}$ and “predicted unstable” means $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^* \geq \hat{\tau}$.

**Step 3: Rank correlation.** Compute Spearman rank correlation $\rho$ between $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ and $p_S^{\mathrm{stab}}$ across the 30 subsets. Expected: strong negative correlation (higher $\mathcal{L}_S^*$ → lower stability).

**Step 4: Theoretical threshold comparison.** Compare the empirically-detected $\hat{\tau}$ against the theoretical $\tau^*$ from Theorem L3 (if the regularity constants $c_u, C_u, C_u’’, B_s, F_s, \kappa_{\min}$ can be estimated or bounded for the specific sampler). Exact numerical match is not expected; order-of-magnitude agreement would be confirmatory.

### Evaluation and Reporting

**Table 3 (Ranked Subset Table):** Per (benchmark, sampler): 30 rows, each giving $S$, $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$, $p_S^{\mathrm{stab}}$, predicted category (easy/hard per Benchmark Summary).

**Figure 4 (Predictivity Scatter):** Per (benchmark, sampler): scatter plot with x-axis $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ and y-axis $p_S^{\mathrm{stab}}$. Expected pattern: monotonically decreasing, potentially with a threshold-like drop around some critical value $\hat{\tau}$.

**Figure 5 (Threshold Detection):** Per (benchmark, sampler): classification accuracy as a function of threshold $\hat{\tau}$, sweeping $\hat{\tau}$ over the range of observed $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ values. The peak gives the best empirical threshold.

**Figure 6 (Per-$|S|$ stratification):** Figure 4 stratified by subset size. Ensures the $\mathcal{L}_S^*$-stability relationship is not confounded by $|S|$.

**Table 4 (Cross-Sampler Comparison):** Spearman $\rho$ and $\hat{\tau}$ for each (benchmark, sampler) combination. Expected: consistent across samplers.

### Falsification Criteria

- **F2.1 (Zero correlation).** $|\rho| < 0.3$ for any benchmark-sampler combination.
- **F2.2 (Inverted correlation).** $\rho > +0.3$ — hard subsets systematically more stable.
- **F2.3 (No threshold behavior).** Figure 5 shows no clear peak in classification accuracy — stability is smoothly distributed across $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ with no threshold-like transition.
- **F2.4 (Cross-sampler inconsistency).** Different category (a) samplers give qualitatively different thresholds or correlations.

**Success criterion:** $|\rho| > 0.7$ in all (benchmark, sampler) combinations, with a visible threshold in Figure 5, consistent across samplers tested.

### Ablation Axes

Since Goal 2 reuses Goal 1’s data, ablations are inherited. No new ablation axes introduced — any Goal 1 ablation (initialization noise, mode separation, sampler choice) automatically produces corresponding Goal 2 analyses.

## Goal 3: Hessian Spectral Check

**Claim tested:** Predictions P3 and P4.

**Central question.** Does Hessian spectral analysis confirm PSD at stable collapsed states and negative eigenvalues at unstable ones?

### Checkpoint Selection

Reuse from Goal 1 (which covers all 30 subsets exhaustively):

- $3$ predicted-stable checkpoints per benchmark (low $\mathcal{L}_S^*$, confirmed stable in Goal 2 analysis).
- $3$ predicted-unstable per benchmark (high $\mathcal{L}_S^*$, confirmed unstable).
- $\sim 4$ additional mid-range checkpoints covering the threshold region.

Total: $\sim 10$ checkpoints per benchmark × $3$ benchmarks = $30$ spectral analyses **per sampler**.

### Hessian-Vector Product Implementation

For direction $v$: $Hv = \nabla_\theta(\nabla_\theta\mathcal{L} \cdot v)$ via double-backward autodiff. Each HVP $\approx 3\times$ gradient evaluation.

**Batch-averaging:** HVP batch size $B_H = 1024$, averaged over $N_{\mathrm{HVP-avg}} = 8$ batches per HVP call.

### Protocol

**Stage 1: Extreme eigenvalues.** Lanczos iteration $T_{\mathrm{lanczos}} = 50$ steps per checkpoint for $\lambda_{\min}$, $\lambda_{\max}$. Convergence check: $T = 40$ vs $T = 50$ within $5%$.

**Stage 2: Eigenvector analysis.** For top-5 smallest and top-5 largest Lanczos eigenvectors $v_i$:

1. Compute $\delta u_i = v_i^\top \nabla_\theta u_\theta$.
1. Compute $\delta\alpha_i$ via $\theta \to \theta + \epsilon_p v_i$ with $\epsilon_p = 0.01$.
1. Classify: **revival** ($|\delta\alpha_i| > 0.01$) or **surviving** ($\leq 0.01$).

**Stage 3: Revival-subspace projection.** Using $T$ from Goal 4, construct $\Pi_{S^c}$. Run Lanczos on projected Hessian $\Pi_{S^c}^\top H \Pi_{S^c}$ for $\lambda_{\min}^{\mathrm{rev}}$.

**Stage 4: Five-term decomposition.** Along minimum-eigenvalue direction, decompose into $P_1, P_2, I_1, I_2, I_3$ (Proposition L2). Monte Carlo with $N_{\mathrm{decomp}} = 5{,}000$ trajectories per term.

### Logged Quantities

Per checkpoint: $\lambda_{\min}$, $\lambda_{\max}$ of full Hessian; $\lambda_{\min}^{\mathrm{rev}}$; top-5 eigenvectors with classification; five-term decomposition along min-eigenvalue direction.

### Evaluation and Reporting

**Table 5 (Spectral Summary):** Per checkpoint: $\hat{\mathcal{L}}*S^*$, $\lambda*{\min}$, $\lambda_{\min}^{\mathrm{rev}}$, classification.

**Figure 7 ($\mathcal{L}*S^*$ vs $\lambda*{\min}^{\mathrm{rev}}$):** Scatter. Expected: $\lambda_{\min}^{\mathrm{rev}} > 0$ below threshold; goes negative above.

**Figure 8 (Term Decomposition):** For 5 representative checkpoints, bar chart $P_1, P_2, |I_1|, |I_2|, |I_3|$. Expected at stable: $P_1 + P_2 \gg |I_1|+|I_2|+|I_3|$.

### Falsification Criteria

- **F3.1 (Negative eigenvalue at stable state):** Checkpoint with $\hat{\mathcal{L}}*S^* \ll \tau^*$ shows $\lambda*{\min}^{\mathrm{rev}} < -10^{-3}$.
- **F3.2 (No PSD/negative distinction):** Similar $\lambda_{\min}$ regardless of $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$.
- **F3.3 (Decomposition inconsistency):** $|I_1|+|I_2|+|I_3| > P_1+P_2$ at stable checkpoints despite $\lambda_{\min} > 0$.

### Ablation Axes

- **3.1 HVP batch size:** $B_H \in {256, 1024, 4096}$.
- **3.2 Lanczos iterations:** $T_{\mathrm{lanczos}} \in {20, 50, 100}$.
- **3.3 Classification perturbation scale:** $\epsilon_p \in {0.001, 0.01, 0.1}$.

## Goal 4: Empirical Verification of Assumption L5’s Consequence

**Claim tested:** Prediction P5.

**Rationale for re-scoping.** Direct L5 — existence of the $\sqrt{\epsilon}$-normalized limit $T\delta\theta$ — is a theoretical construct not directly measurable at the exact collapsed state. We verify the *consequence* appearing in the theorem: $P_1 \geq c_u |\delta\alpha|^2$.

### Checkpoint Selection

Same $\sim 30$ checkpoints per sampler as Goal 3.

### Protocol

**Stage 1: Perturbation direction generation.** For each checkpoint:

- Sample $N_{\mathrm{dir}} = 200$ random unit-norm directions $v_j$ (standard Gaussian, normalized).
- Scale: $\delta\theta_j = \epsilon_{\mathrm{pert}} v_j$ with $\epsilon_{\mathrm{pert}} = 0.05$.

**Stage 2: Per-perturbation measurement.** For each $\delta\theta_j$:

*Measure $P_1$:*
$$\hat{P_1}(\delta\theta_j) = \frac{1}{N_{\mathrm{traj}}} \sum_{i=1}^{N_{\mathrm{traj}}} \int_0^1 |(\delta\theta_j^\top \nabla_\theta u_\theta)(X_t^{(i)}, t)|^2,dt$$
with $N_{\mathrm{traj}} = 1{,}000$.

*Measure $\delta\alpha$:* Sample $N_{\mathrm{eval}} = 10{,}000$ trajectories from $Q_{\theta + \delta\theta_j}$ and $Q_\theta$:
$$\hat{\delta\alpha}*j = (\alpha_k^{(\theta + \delta\theta_j)} - \alpha_k^{(\theta)})*{k \notin S}.$$

*Classify:* Revival if $|\hat{\delta\alpha}*j| > \alpha*{\mathrm{revival}} = 0.005$; else surviving.

**Stage 3: Scatter and fit.** Restrict to revival directions. Scatter $\hat{P_1}$ vs $|\hat{\delta\alpha}|^2$. Theoretical prediction: all points above $y = c_u^{\mathrm{theory}} x$ with
$$c_u^{\mathrm{theory}} = \frac{1}{|S^c|,g_{\max}^2} = \frac{1}{|S^c| \cdot 9}.$$
Extract:
$$c_u^{\mathrm{emp}} := \min_j \hat{P_1}_j / |\hat{\delta\alpha}_j|^2.$$

### Logged Quantities

Per checkpoint: $N_{\mathrm{dir}} = 200$ raw measurements $(\hat{P_1}_j, |\hat{\delta\alpha}_j|^2, \text{revival flag})$; $c_u^{\mathrm{emp}}$, $c_u^{\mathrm{theory}}$, ratio; revival-classification fraction.

### Evaluation and Reporting

**Figure 9 ($P_1$ vs $|\delta\alpha|^2$ Scatter):** Per checkpoint with theoretical lower-bound line overlaid. Expected: all revival points above the line.

**Table 6 (Per-checkpoint L5 Check):** $c_u^{\mathrm{theory}}$, $c_u^{\mathrm{emp}}$, ratio, violation fraction.

**Figure 10 (Distribution of $c_u^{\mathrm{emp}}$):** Histogram across all checkpoints. Expected bounded away from zero.

### Falsification Criteria

- **F4.1 (Below-theory violations):** More than $5%$ of revival perturbations show $\hat{P_1}/|\hat{\delta\alpha}|^2 < c_u^{\mathrm{theory}}$.
- **F4.2 (Zero-slope directions):** Revival direction with $|\hat{\delta\alpha}| > \alpha_{\mathrm{revival}}$ but $\hat{P_1} < 0.01$.
- **F4.3 (Non-linear scaling):** Scatter clearly sub-linear instead of predicted quadratic relationship.

### Ablation Axes

- **4.1 Perturbation scale:** $\epsilon_{\mathrm{pert}} \in {0.01, 0.05, 0.1, 0.2}$.
- **4.2 Number of directions:** $N_{\mathrm{dir}} \in {50, 200, 1000}$.
- **4.3 Direction-generation method:** Random Gaussian vs structured (Hessian principal components from Goal 3).

## Goal 5: Step-Size Escape (Training Dynamics Implications)

**Claim tested:** Prediction P6.

**Rationale for scope.** Theorem L3 establishes $\theta_S^*$ as a local minimum of the loss as a function. Corollary A1 adds GD convergence requires $\eta < 2/\lambda_{\max}$. Escape at large step size is a training-dynamics phenomenon, distinct from loss-landscape instability.

### Checkpoint Selection

$3$ stable checkpoints per benchmark × $3$ benchmarks = $9$ starting points **per sampler**. All verified stable (Goal 1) with PSD Hessian (Goal 3) and known $\lambda_{\max}$.

### Protocol

**Stage 1: Step-size sweep.** For each checkpoint, baseline $\eta_0 = 1/\lambda_{\max}$ (well inside $\eta < 2/\lambda_{\max}$). Sweep:
$$\eta \in {0.5, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 2.5, 3.0, 4.0, 6.0} \times \eta_0.$$

**Stage 2: Continuation training.** For each $(\text{checkpoint}, \eta)$:

- Initialize at stable checkpoint.
- Train $300$ epochs at constant $\eta$ with no inner-loop evaluation.
- Evaluate mode weights every $20$ epochs (outside training loop).
- $N_{\mathrm{seed}} = 5$ seeds per pair.

**Stage 3: Escape classification.** Per run:

- **No escape:** $\mathrm{MWD}(e) < 0.05$ at all evaluation points in $e \in [20, 300]$.
- **Escape:** $\mathrm{MWD}(e) > 0.15$ for at least $3$ consecutive evaluation points.
- **Oscillation:** $\mathrm{MWD}(e)$ crosses $0.1$ multiple times without converging.

$P_{\mathrm{escape}}(\eta) = $ fraction of seeds with escape classification.

**Stage 4: Critical step size.** $\eta^* = $ smallest $\eta$ where $P_{\mathrm{escape}}(\eta) > 0.5$. Compare to theoretical $\eta_{\mathrm{th}} = 2/\lambda_{\max}$.

**Total compute per sampler:** $9$ checkpoints × $8$ step sizes × $5$ seeds × $300$ epochs = $108{,}000$ epochs. Scales linearly with number of samplers tested.

### Logged Quantities

**During training:** Training loss at each epoch.

**At evaluation points (every 20 epochs):** Mode weights from 10k-sample estimation; MWD derived.

**Post-training:** Final mode weights for escaped runs (to classify reached attractor).

### Evaluation and Reporting

**Figure 11 (Escape Probability Curves):** Per checkpoint, $P_{\mathrm{escape}}(\eta)$ vs $\eta/\eta_0$ with vertical line at $\eta_{\mathrm{th}}$. Expected sharp transition near $\eta_{\mathrm{th}}$.

**Table 7 (Critical Step Size vs Theoretical):** $\lambda_{\max}$, $\eta_{\mathrm{th}}$, $\eta^*$, ratio $\eta^*/\eta_{\mathrm{th}}$. Expected ratio near $1$.

**Figure 12 (Post-Escape Attractor):** For escaped runs, bar chart of reached subsets. Tests whether escape routes cluster on specific “nearby” stable attractors.

### Falsification Criteria

- **F5.1 (No step-size effect):** $P_{\mathrm{escape}}(\eta)$ near zero even at $\eta = 6\eta_0$.
- **F5.2 (Escape at small step size):** $P_{\mathrm{escape}}(\eta) > 0.5$ at $\eta < 0.5\eta_0$.
- **F5.3 (No correlation with $\lambda_{\max}$):** $\eta^*/\eta_{\mathrm{th}}$ varies wildly across checkpoints.

### Ablation Axes

- **5.1 Optimizer:** Plain SGD, momentum SGD ($\beta = 0.9$), Adam.
- **5.2 Schedule:** Constant, cosine decay, linear warmup.
- **5.3 Batch size:** ${64, 256, 1024}$.

## Summary and Cross-Goal Connections

The five goals form a logical chain:

1. **Goal 1** establishes that some collapsed states are stable, with exhaustive coverage of all 30 subsets per benchmark. This produces the full $(\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*, \text{stability})$ dataset.
1. **Goal 2** is a re-analysis of Goal 1’s data: does $\hat{\mathcal{L}}_S^*$ ranking predict stability? No new training required.
1. **Goal 3** verifies the Hessian mechanism directly via spectral analysis on stable and unstable checkpoints from Goal 1.
1. **Goal 4** verifies the key assumption L5 empirically on the same Goal 3 checkpoints.
1. **Goal 5** explores training-dynamics implications on stable Goal 1 checkpoints.

All experiments should be run on **multiple category (a) samplers** independently (at least two) — see `category_a_samplers.md` for specific architectures and losses. Cross-sampler consistency is an additional robustness check.

**Compute budget per sampler (in epochs):**

- Goal 1: $\sim 135{,}000$ epochs (30 subsets × 500 pre-training + 30 × 200 × 5 stability × 3 benchmarks).
- Goal 2: No new training.
- Goal 3: $\sim 30$ spectral analyses (Lanczos + decomposition), cheap relative to training.
- Goal 4: $\sim 30$ checkpoints × $200$ directions × Monte Carlo, moderate cost.
- Goal 5: $\sim 108{,}000$ epochs (9 checkpoints × 8 step sizes × 5 seeds × 300 epochs).

Budget scales linearly with the number of samplers tested. Since these are 2D benchmarks with light training, total compute should be tractable on modest hardware over several weeks.

Taken together, the goals test: (a) whether the phenomenon exists (Goal 1), (b) whether the theory predicts correctly (Goal 2), (c) whether the theoretical mechanism holds (Goal 3), (d) whether the key assumption is valid (Goal 4), and (e) what the practical implications are (Goal 5). Cross-sampler consistency provides an additional dimension of robustness.
